The heart of our village is under threat

The King's Arms: At the Heart of Polebrook

The King's Arms is a striking late 17th-century Grade II Listed building, built from coursed limestone and crowned with a traditional reed-thatched roof. Standing at the centre of Polebrook's conservation area, it is one of the village's most recognisable and cherished landmarks.

For over three centuries, it has been a place of welcome, gathering, and shared life.

More Than a Pub

For centuries, the King's Arms was far more than a public house. From the 18th century onwards, it served as the village's informal administrative heart, hosting land auctions, acting as a stop for mail collection, and even functioning as a courtroom for local Petty Sessions.

This unique history makes the King's Arms not just a place of hospitality, but a living record of Polebrook's civic and social life.

What's Happening Now

Today, the King’s Arms remains the only community space in the village open on a near-daily basis. It is the go-to venue for local meetings, social gatherings, celebrations, and village events, as well as a much-loved dining and drinking destination.

Its potential closure would leave Polebrook without a walkable, shared social space.

What We Will Lose

Losing the King’s Arms would cause genuine social hardship. For many residents, particularly older or less mobile members of the community, the pub is their primary, and sometimes only, source of regular social interaction.

This would be more than the loss of an amenity. It would be the loss of connection, inclusion, and everyday community life.

What We Can Do

With your support, we can protect the King’s Arms and preserve it as the historic and social lifeblood of Polebrook, not just for today, but for generations to come.
